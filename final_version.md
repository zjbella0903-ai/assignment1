Title: Personalized Learning with Generative AI

Hi, I’m Bella, a JHU master’s student in ISAI. I focus on building usable AI systems that improve everyday learning and work.

I’m especially interested in education technology, specifically personalized learning assistants. This area matters because learners have diverse backgrounds and pacing needs, but instructors and TAs have limited time. Well‑designed AI support can help students get timely feedback while giving instructors better insights into progress and misconceptions.

Where generative AI might help (3 ways):

Idea 1 — Feedback copilot for assignments: Provide rubric‑aligned, step‑by‑step hints (not final answers), flag common errors, and suggest resources tailored to each student’s history. This can reduce hint requests per student by 20% after two weeks.
Idea 2 — Learning‑path personalization: Summarize what a student knows from prior work, then generate micro‑lessons, quiz questions, and practice exercises at the right difficulty level, updating the plan as the learner improves.
Idea 3 — Instructor analytics and content drafting: Turn messy class data (forum threads, submissions, quiz results) into concise dashboards highlighting at‑risk students and frequent misconceptions, and draft lecture notes or examples the instructor can refine.
